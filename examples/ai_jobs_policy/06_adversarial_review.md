# 06 Adversarial Review

## Headline Finding Scores

- Targeted transition compact over blanket tax: 8/10. Supported by `C-006`, `C-010`, `C-014`, and `C-025`, but country-specific design remains open.
- Pure laissez-faire is too weak: 8/10. Supported by `C-001`, `C-020`, and `C-024`, with caveat that robot evidence is not identical to generative AI.
- AI exposure is not realized job loss: 9/10. Supported by `C-003`, `C-004`, `C-005`, `C-013`, and `C-021`.
- Broad AI automation tax lacks evaluated support in this run: 7/10. Supported by run-local negative evidence `C-026`, but limited by search scope.

## Weakest Link

`C-026` is the weakest headline support because a null retrieval result is not a universal absence proof. The specific evidence needed is a primary government evaluation or quasi-experimental study of a broad AI automation tax or slowdown rule and its employment effects.

## Bias Check

The synthesis leans toward economist and practitioner feasibility concerns, but the academic and historian lenses prevent a productivity-only conclusion. The skeptic lens is useful because it keeps model results and exposure estimates from being over-interpreted.

## Missing Perspective

A labor-organizing or worker-voice lens would add bargaining-power evidence, union strategy, worker surveillance concerns, and political legitimacy. That could change the design of transition support even if it would not by itself justify a blanket tax.

## Integrity Audit

- Citation integrity: all supported factual and inference claims cite source IDs.
- Scope: exposure estimates are not converted into job-loss forecasts.
- Source concentration: IMF and institutional sources carry much of the policy package; peer-reviewed robot and task evidence carries displacement and theory.
- Recommendations: the main recommendation cites both source-backed claims and unresolved contradictions.
- Time sensitivity: current observed-disruption claims are explicitly near-term and do not settle medium-term risk.

## Publication and Content Verification Audit

- `S-002`, `S-007`, `S-008`, and `S-011` were used at abstract or metadata level where full text was not retrieved; claims relying on them are scoped and non-causal.
- Direct-support claims have evidence IDs and locator fields.
- `03_evidence_verdicts.jsonl` includes verdict records for the located claim/evidence pairs.
- No source is marked retracted or superseded in the artifacts.
- `C-008` and `C-023` explicitly limit robot-tax theory rather than treating it as implementation evidence for generative AI.

## Overall Note

A tough external reviewer would likely grade this as B+/A-. The main fix before government use would be country-specific incidence modeling and primary evidence on any implemented automation-tax instrument. The brief is publishable as an example run because it exposes its retrieval limits and unresolved contradictions.
