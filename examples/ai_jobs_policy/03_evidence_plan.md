# 03 Evidence Plan

Live web retrieval was used because the question depends on current policy analysis and source-traceable evidence. Academic MCP tools were not callable in this environment, so the run fell back to web search and direct official URLs. Source identity is marked according to what was actually retrieved.

## Lens Queries and Disconfirming Evidence

### Academic

- Query peer-reviewed robot and task-based automation evidence.
- Disconfirming evidence: high-quality evidence that AI exposure has already produced broad aggregate job loss, or that robot evidence has no relevance to AI labor risk.

### Economist

- Query robot-tax theory, tax-code automation bias, and IMF fiscal-policy guidance.
- Disconfirming evidence: evaluated evidence that broad AI taxes protect jobs without reducing beneficial adoption or creating administrative distortions.

### Practitioner

- Query implementation-oriented institutional reports and current labor-market trackers.
- Disconfirming evidence: a clear, tested administrative method for distinguishing labor-substituting AI from augmenting AI at scale.

### Historian

- Query long-run technology-and-jobs reports and systematic reviews.
- Disconfirming evidence: evidence that generative AI adoption produces adjustment patterns unrelated to prior technology transitions.

### Skeptic

- Query null evidence, caveats, and model-to-policy limits.
- Disconfirming evidence: primary evaluation of a broad AI automation tax protecting workers under current AI adoption.
