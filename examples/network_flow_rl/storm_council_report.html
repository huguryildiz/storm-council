01Bottom line

Use RL selectively, not as a general replacement for network-flow optimization. Classical flow solvers and traffic-engineering optimizers remain the baseline for feasibility-critical or auditable decisions. RL is worth evaluating as a bounded augmentation for dynamic traffic engineering, candidate selection, learned warm starts, or learning-accelerated optimization, provided a solver/shield enforces hard constraints and a shadow-mode benchmark proves value on real traffic.

04Decision options & trade-offs

evidence: strong

A · Keep classical optimization only

  • Best fit for stable/offline flow optimization and auditable hard constraints.
  • Lowest operational complexity and easiest rollback.

When appropriate: Static planning, compliance-sensitive routing, or teams without ML operations capacity.

evidence: moderate

B · Hybrid learning-accelerated optimization

  • Uses learning for warm starts, ranking, or acceleration while retaining solver-backed constraints.
  • Needs benchmark harness and MLOps ownership.

When appropriate: Large or repeated TE problems where solver latency is meaningful.

evidence: moderate

C · Guarded RL for dynamic traffic engineering

  • Potential value for critical-flow selection, load balancing, and rapid response to traffic changes.
  • Must run in shadow or advisory mode first with explicit safety shields.

When appropriate: High-volume online TE where real-traffic replay shows value over incumbent optimizers.

unsupported

D · End-to-end RL replacement

  • Does not preserve enough evidence of constraint guarantees, production transfer, or rollback safety.
  • Not recommended on current evidence.

When appropriate: Research exploration only, not production control.

05Recommended next actions

  • Classify the target workload as offline flow planning, online traffic engineering, datacenter scheduling, or incident response. X-002 C-042
  • Build a replay benchmark using real traffic matrices, topology snapshots, and failure cases. X-003 C-032
  • Compare RL against min-cost/max-flow, LP/CP/TE optimizers, and the incumbent heuristic before any production decision. C-001 C-012 C-033
  • Require a solver, projection layer, or shield to enforce hard constraints in any RL architecture. X-004 C-020 C-023
  • Start with shadow mode or bounded advisory deployment; promote only after measured gains and rollback tests. C-023 C-043 X-005

06Evidence gaps & frontier questions

  • The target organization's traffic distributions, topology changes, and failure patterns are unknown. X-003
  • Residual incident risk after adding RL has not been quantified. X-004
  • Total cost of ownership is workload-specific and not directly resolved by the literature. X-005
  • The decision should be narrowed from generic network flow optimization to one concrete workload. C-042
