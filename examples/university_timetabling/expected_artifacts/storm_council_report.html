01Bottom line

On current evidence, reinforcement learning should not replace CP-SAT/MIP for university course timetabling. Exact and metaheuristic methods reliably satisfy hard constraints and produce auditable schedules, whereas RL's demonstrated strengths in combinatorial optimization come mostly from routing/packing studies and rarely beat strong OR baselines on constrained scheduling. RL's plausible near-term role is narrow: augmenting soft or dynamic objectives (e.g. disruption rescheduling), and only where a validated simulator/reward and a reproducible baseline exist — neither of which the literature currently provides for this domain. The decision is therefore inconclusive in RL's favour: keep solver-based timetabling, optionally pilot learning-augmented OR, and commission the missing benchmark before any RL investment.

04Decision options & trade-offs

evidence: strong

A · Keep CP-SAT/MIP as primary; no RL

  • Proven feasibility/optimality, auditable, lowest total cost of ownership.
  • Limited adaptivity to soft or dynamic objectives.

When appropriate: most universities with stable, constraint-heavy schedules.

evidence: moderate

B · CP-SAT/MIP primary + pilot learning-augmented OR

  • Keeps optimality guarantees; evidence-backed direction; incremental and reversible.
  • Engineering effort; uncertain marginal gains; needs ML capacity.

When appropriate: large institutions with ML capacity seeking efficiency gains.

evidence: weak

C · Add an RL layer for dynamic rescheduling only (guarded pilot)

  • Potential labour savings during disruptions.
  • Requires a validated simulator/reward; governance & explainability risk; no domain benchmark exists.

When appropriate: only as a pre-registered pilot with a CP-SAT baseline and human oversight.

unsupported

D · Replace the solver with end-to-end RL

  • No optimality guarantee; no domain evidence; high operational and trust risk.

When appropriate: not recommended on current evidence.

05Recommended next actions

  • Commission or locate an ITC2007-based RL-vs-CP-SAT benchmark before any RL investment. X-005
  • Quantify per-incident rescheduling labour and disruption frequency by institution type. X-004
  • Prototype learning-augmented OR (learned branching / warm-starts) feeding CP-SAT (Option B).
  • If exploring RL, first build and validate a disruption simulator and reward, with explicit governance for student-facing automation.
  • Keep CP-SAT/MIP as the system of record throughout.

06Evidence gaps & frontier questions

  • No peer-reviewed head-to-head of RL vs CP-SAT/MIP on university course timetabling. X-005
  • No validated simulator/reward for institution-specific disruption dynamics. X-001
  • No data on per-incident rescheduling labour or disruption frequency. X-004
  • Open question: can learned components reliably warm-start CP-SAT at university scale, and what reward avoids proxy-objective drift?
