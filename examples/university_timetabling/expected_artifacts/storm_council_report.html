01Bottom line

On current evidence, reinforcement learning should not replace CP-SAT/MIP for university course timetabling. Exact and metaheuristic methods reliably satisfy hard constraints and produce auditable schedules, whereas RL's demonstrated strengths in combinatorial optimization come mostly from routing/packing studies and rarely beat strong OR baselines on constrained scheduling. RL's plausible near-term role is narrow: augmenting soft or dynamic objectives (e.g. disruption rescheduling), and only where a validated simulator/reward and a reproducible baseline exist — neither of which the literature currently provides for this domain. The decision is therefore inconclusive in RL's favour: keep solver-based timetabling, optionally pilot learning-augmented OR, and commission the missing benchmark before any RL investment.

02The five council lenses

practitioner

Judge whether an approach works in production, not just on paper. Weigh operational constraints, implementation reality, failure modes, and adoption friction.

Priority questions
  • How are university timetables actually produced in production today?
  • Where do current solver-based systems fail or require manual fixing?
  • What operational burden would an RL component add?
Expected evidence

Deployment case studies; Solver documentation; Operational post-mortems

Likely blind spots

Discounting research advances that have not yet been productized.

Potential conflicts

Tension with the academic view on how mature RL methods are.

Escalation triggers

A claim that RL is production-ready without deployment evidence.

academic

Ground claims in peer-reviewed evidence. Examine theoretical assumptions, reproducibility, benchmark quality, and methodological limits.

Priority questions
  • What does peer-reviewed evidence show about RL for combinatorial optimization?
  • Are there standardized benchmarks comparing RL to CP-SAT/MIP on timetabling?
  • What are the methodological limits of the RL-CO literature?
Expected evidence

Peer-reviewed surveys; Benchmark results; Reproducibility studies

Likely blind spots

Under-weighting deployment and maintenance realities.

Potential conflicts

May find practitioner optimism unsupported by benchmarks.

Escalation triggers

Claims citing non-reproducible or vendor-only results.

skeptic

Stress-test claims. Look for unsupported assertions, alternative explanations, weak assumptions, incentive problems, and missing or negative evidence.

Priority questions
  • Which optimistic claims lack reproducible baselines?
  • What incentives might inflate reported RL gains?
  • Is absence of head-to-head evidence being glossed over?
Expected evidence

Reproductions and failures; Baseline comparisons; Negative results

Likely blind spots

Reflexive dismissal of genuinely novel methods.

Potential conflicts

Challenges both practitioner and academic optimism.

Escalation triggers

A recommendation resting on a single low-quality source.

economist

Reason about cost, incentives, externalities, opportunity cost, and distributional effects. Ask who pays, who benefits, and what is foregone.

Priority questions
  • What is the total cost of ownership of an RL approach vs CP-SAT?
  • What is the opportunity cost of ML effort spent on timetabling?
  • Who bears the switching and adoption costs?
Expected evidence

Cost models; Labor estimates; Adoption/procurement data

Likely blind spots

Quantifying only what is measurable, missing soft value.

Potential conflicts

Internal tension between opportunity cost and resilience value.

Escalation triggers

A payoff claim with no quantified labor or cost basis.

historian

Bring precedent and institutional memory. Identify historical analogies, repeated failure patterns, and the integration/trust dynamics that decide adoption.

Priority questions
  • How have previous 'autonomous scheduling' waves fared?
  • Why did competition-winning methods not always deploy?
  • What recurring failure patterns apply to RL here?
Expected evidence

Historical surveys; Retrospectives; Institutional case histories

Likely blind spots

Assuming the past fully constrains a genuinely new method.

Potential conflicts

May read current RL enthusiasm as a repeat of past hype cycles.

Escalation triggers

Replacement framing that ignores integration/trust history.

08Decision options & trade-offs

evidence: strong

A · Keep CP-SAT/MIP as primary; no RL

  • Proven feasibility/optimality, auditable, lowest total cost of ownership.
  • Limited adaptivity to soft or dynamic objectives.

When appropriate: most universities with stable, constraint-heavy schedules.

evidence: moderate

B · CP-SAT/MIP primary + pilot learning-augmented OR

  • Keeps optimality guarantees; evidence-backed direction; incremental and reversible.
  • Engineering effort; uncertain marginal gains; needs ML capacity.

When appropriate: large institutions with ML capacity seeking efficiency gains.

evidence: weak

C · Add an RL layer for dynamic rescheduling only (guarded pilot)

  • Potential labour savings during disruptions.
  • Requires a validated simulator/reward; governance & explainability risk; no domain benchmark exists.

When appropriate: only as a pre-registered pilot with a CP-SAT baseline and human oversight.

unsupported

D · Replace the solver with end-to-end RL

  • No optimality guarantee; no domain evidence; high operational and trust risk.

When appropriate: not recommended on current evidence.

09Recommended next actions

  • Commission or locate an ITC2007-based RL-vs-CP-SAT benchmark before any RL investment. X-005
  • Quantify per-incident rescheduling labour and disruption frequency by institution type. X-004
  • Prototype learning-augmented OR (learned branching / warm-starts) feeding CP-SAT (Option B).
  • If exploring RL, first build and validate a disruption simulator and reward, with explicit governance for student-facing automation.
  • Keep CP-SAT/MIP as the system of record throughout.

10Evidence gaps & frontier questions

  • No peer-reviewed head-to-head of RL vs CP-SAT/MIP on university course timetabling. X-005
  • No validated simulator/reward for institution-specific disruption dynamics. X-001
  • No data on per-incident rescheduling labour or disruption frequency. X-004
  • Open question: can learned components reliably warm-start CP-SAT at university scale, and what reward avoids proxy-objective drift?
